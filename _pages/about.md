Hazrat Ali is a Lecturer in Artificial Intelligence and Data Science at the [University of Stirling](https://www.cs.stir.ac.uk/). He is an experienced educator and researcher with over a decade of experience in the field, including working as a researcher at the Biomedical Engineering R&D centre of [Umea University Sweden](https://www.umu.se/en/) and [Hamad Bin Khalifa University Qatar](https://www.hbku.edu.qa/en). His extensive research portfolio spans various areas of Generative AI, Medical AI, healthcare, and computer vision, with a specific focus on generative adversarial networks for medical imaging, deep learning for ultrasound medical imaging, and AI for healthcare. He has developed AI methods for medical images for applications such as GANs for disease transformation, GANs for medical imaging super-resolutions, and deep learning for motor unit segmentation, which have been widely adopted and cited by the research community. He made contributions to deep learning for ultrasound imaging and the VIPVIZA projects at Umea University Sweden. In addition, he has developed open-source datasets, including a dataset of natural scene images with Urdu text, the **UHaT dataset** (the first dataset on Urdu handwritten characters), and a speech corpus for Urdu. These datasets, available on the [Kaggle platform](https://www.kaggle.com/hazrat/datasets), have proven to be invaluable resources for AI-oriented tasks and have contributed to advancing the field of AI. 

He has authored more than 70 peer-reviewed articles published in top-tier international journals and conferences including IEEE Transactions on Image Processing, Nature Scientific Reports, IEEE CVPR workshop, and NeurIPS workshops. He is a senior member of IEEE, Fellow of the Advance-HE, a Global Ambassador at the **Global Council for Responsible AI**, and a former member of the Swedish AI Society. He is the lead editor of the book [**Advances in Deep Generative Models for Medical AI**](https://link.springer.com/book/10.1007/978-3-031-46341-9) published by Springer Nature and currently editing a book [**Large Language Models in Healthcare**](https://www.routledge.com/Applications-of-Large-Language-Models-LLM-in-Healthcare-Systems-Opportunities-Challenges-and-Ethical-Considerations/Zamanifar-Faezipour-SoleimanianGharehchopogh-Farhadi/p/book/9781041124917), to be published by CRC press in 2026. He serves as Associate Editor at Wiley Applied AI Letters, Nature Scientific Reports, Wiley Engineering Reports, IEEE and as Academic Editor at PeerJ Computer Science. He is lead guest editor for special issue on Multimodal AI for Healthcare. He has organized and/or chaired several workshops/conferences including the [Workshop on Foundation AI Models in Biomedical Imaging](https://hazratali.github.io/faibi2026/) at the IEEE International Conference on Biomedical Imaging in Texas (USA) in 2025 and in Londin in 2026, a special session on [AI in Surgery](https://hazratali.github.io/aisur/) at the UK premier conference on [Medical Imaging Understanding and Analysis (MIUA 2025)](https://conferences.leeds.ac.uk/miua/) in Leeds, and a special session on [Multimodal Generative AI in Healthcare](https://aiih.cc/) at the AI in Healthcare conference (AIiH 2025) in Cambridge (2025) and London (2026). He has served as session chair at Machine Intelligence and Signal Processing (MISP 2022). He has been the lead organizer of the 2021 NeurIPS meet-up in Doha, Qatar and the 2020 meet-up in Umea, Sweden. He has served as Area Chair at IEEE International Joint Confefence on Neural Networks (IEEE IJCNN 2026 and IJCNN 2025), Area Chair at the IEEE International Conference on Acoustics, Speech, and Signal Processing (ICASSP 2026), and served as a PC member at leading international conferences including IEEE International Conference on Artificial Intelligence ICAI 2024, IEEE CVPR WiCV 2026, WiCV 2026, WiCV2024, and MICCAI. He has delivered invited talks at various national and international venues including Newcastle University, St. John University New York USA, Biomedical Engineering R and D center of Umea University Sweden, HBKU Graduate Seminar Qatar, and International Conference on Computational Sciences and Technology (INCCST 2019). 

He was selected as a young researcher at the 5th Heidelberg Laureate Forum, Heidelberg, Germany. He has twice received the HLF Foundation Travel Award for participation in the [Heidelberg Laureate Forum](https://www.heidelberg-laureate-forum.org/) (2017 as a young researcher and 2026 as an HLF alumni). He is the recipient of the 2021 [DAAD AI Networking Fellowship Germany](https://www.daad.de/en/the-daad/postdocnet/fellows/fellows/), the Best Researcher Award by COMSATS University (2020-2021), and the Kempe Foundation Sweden grant for postdoc research (2020), the HEC PhD Scholarship, the UKOMAIN travel bursary 2026 and 2025 (An EPRSRC network), the AIBIO-UK Travel Award for the AIBIO-UK 3rd Annual Conference 2026 in Aberdeen (a BBSRC network), the UKRI Digital Research Skills Catalyst travel scholarship for AICatalyst2026 conference in York, UK, Google DeepMind MenaML winter school travel grant 2026, the travel scholarship grant by European Machine Learning school 2017, the 2018 Top 10 research pitch award by the University of Queensland, Australia, IEEE Student Travel Award, the 2017 IBRO grant, the 2023-24 USTB excellent international student award, and the 2013-14 Erasmus Mundus STRoNGTiES research grant.
